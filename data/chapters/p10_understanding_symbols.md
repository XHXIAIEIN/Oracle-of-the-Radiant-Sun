# Understanding Symbols

Ever since the first cave dwellers looked on the ground, saw the paw prints of a sabre-tooth tiger, and hotfooted it to the nearest tree, mankind has read symbols. The marks that appear on the computer screen as I type conjure up visual images that transfer themselves meaningfully to you in a flash as you read them. Depending on how evocatively I write, your mind will be excited or bored. Unlike a computer, however, the mind does not simply store each piece of information received, ready to be extracted and repeated verbatim. As soon as information arrives in your mental databank, it undergoes a transformation. It is applied to and compared with other stored information, and enhanced and made relevant to a thousand criteria that make up your own belief system, philosophy and wealth of personal experience. From all this, you judge and reframe the received material, and then pass it on colored with your own unique interpretation.

## Reading the Future

Divination is based on a similar inspirational reading of symbols. This comes rarely in the form of words, however, and more often involves physical observation and created images. Observed events in nature that predict future events can range from the tiger's footprint to weather forecasting, from astrology to the predicting of stock-market turns and economic cycles.

The human mind is astounding in its ability to make brilliant leaps of imagination and achieve accurate observations. For the first thirty or so years of photography in the nineteenth century, people were perfectly happy to look at black and white images of their loved ones. They did not see the images simply as a symbolic representation of degrees of light and shade – they saw the person. This ability to transfer images immediately into mental reality has produced mankind's greatest visual system for understanding: language.
