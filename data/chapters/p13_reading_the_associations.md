# Reading the Associations

Any of the combinations of House, sign and planet in the table opposite can be used to describe people and events, as in the examples below.

## Sample Charts

Looking at the birthchart for someone with Sun in Scorpio in the Third House, taking the keywords detailed in the chart gives the following clues:

| **Sun** | + | **Scorpio** | + | **Third House** |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| *drive to succeed* | | *passion/self-mastery* | | *mental ability<br>communication* |

Expanded, this gives the following interpretation: this chart describes someone with a powerful and self-controlled ability to achieve authority through writing, talking and other forms of communication. This may suggest that the person is an author or orator.

If the reading refers to an event rather than a person, it could be interpreted as follows: an intense, concentrated study of a creative subject with a view to enhancing the ability to communicate. Perhaps it involves throwing great energy into learning a language or acquiring IT skills.

Every one of us has a personal astrological birthchart that is virtually unique, and within your chart are a dozen or so of these simple combinations. When translated, they combine to create a very good description of the unique person you are. And they are more accurate and succinct than magazine sun-sign definitions such as “Librans can't make up their minds” or “All Scorpios have a sting in the tail.”

While most modern astrology is geared to interpreting birthcharts, another form of the art, known as Horary astrology, is becoming popular as more astrologers discover how effective it can be. Horary casts a chart for the exact time a question is asked, and the chart is then interpreted according to a strict set of rules. Using the same basic material, it can give surprisingly apt answers to questions. Horary astrology works with the planets Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn only, and for this reason we use the same seven celestial objects in this Sun oracle.
