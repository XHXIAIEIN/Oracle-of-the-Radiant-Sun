# The Astrological Houses

The astrological chart has twelve Houses; each is the natural home of and ruled by a sign and a planet. To recap, each House governs a certain realm of life experience, each planet is associated with particular functions or actions, and each sign influences the nature or style of an action. The Sun and Moon rule one House each; other planets rule two.

## Reading The Zodiac Wheel

Look at the zodiac-wheel diagram; think of the three concentric rings as separate dials that, at any time, can be (and nearly always are) displaced in a spontaneous reading. Planets will be aligned with different signs, and signs sit in varying Houses. This displacement gives clues to interpret.

## Sample Reading

To interpret the setting of the dial as it relates to a particular event, recall the lists of qualities detailed on page 12. Taking Mars in Gemini in the Second House as a reading, for example, we must remember that:

- The First House, Aries and Mars all relate to self-expression.
- The Second House, Taurus and Venus all relate to possessions and material senses.
- The Third House, Gemini and Mercury all relate to communication, and so on.

So for Mars in Gemini in the Second House, we have to combine three sets of associations. As Mars in Gemini is about decision-making and the Second House concerns possessions, this combination can be interpreted as requiring a quick decision with regard to possessions or money.

## The Houses in Horary Astrology

When you make a Horary chart with the Sun oracle, each House governs specific questions and this is the place to look for the answer to a reading. Below are detailed the specific areas of life ruled by the twelve Houses.

## The associations of each House

> *Diagram transcription.* A circle divided into twelve numbered segments. The keywords for each House are listed beside the diagram, not inside the segments:

1. The questioner
2. Property, finances
3. Brothers, sisters, communication
4. Parents, home, family
5. Children, creative matters, love affairs
6. Work, health, service
7. Marriage, business partnerships
8. Shared property, others' finances
9. Religion, philosophy, law, distant travel
10. Career, place in community
11. Friends, associations, hopes and wishes
12. Spirituality, institutions, secret enemies

### First House

Questions associated with the First House shed light on the state of mind of the questioner, and his or her health and circumstances. This House also indicates how the enquirer sees him or herself, and how others regard him or her.

### Second House

Second-house questions are about finances, wealth, poverty, loss or gains. This House describes possessions in the portable sense, such as furniture, art, jewelry and money used in speculation. It can also indicate one's physical abilities and talents as well as anything that can be described as one's “property.”

### Third House

The Third House offers information on communications of all kinds, including letters, messages and documents. This House also represents brothers and sisters and describes sibling relationships. Questions related to everyday travel, knowledge, early learning, short journeys, mental energy and telepathy can also be answered by looking at cards drawn at the Third House position.

### Fourth House

Questions of the Fourth House concern home and family. This House describes events and happenings that affect one's father or mother, house, family, land, estates and gardens. The Fourth House also describes the end or the summing up of the question.

### Fifth House

The Fifth House deals in questions about children, pregnancy, lotteries, gambling, love affairs and anything involving creativity. This can include artistic pursuits, hobbies, recreation, children and lovers.

### Sixth House

Sixth-house questions concern employees, workers and day-to-day life. Also matters relating to health, service to others, small animals and pets.

### Seventh House

Questions associated with the Seventh House relate to a husband or wife, long-term partners, marriages, business partnerships, lawsuits, contracts, agreements and disagreements with others. This House also refers to matters concerning nieces, nephews and grandparents.

### Eighth House

This House sheds light on questions to do with legacies, inheritances, shared finances, other people's money, a partner's money or possessions, and the health or work of brothers and sisters. Traditionally, this is the house of death, but in the broadest sense, this concerns the ending of one thing in order to make room for another.

### Ninth House

Ninth-house questions concern beliefs, philosophy, overseas travel, foreign places, higher learning, academic subjects, law, religion, publishing, foreign culture and sport.

### Tenth House

Questions for this House pertain to one's public life, standing in the community, career, social status and attitudes to parenting. It represents honor, authority, career and professions, life direction and achievement.

### Eleventh House

Questions relating to the Eleventh House concern friendships and one's relationships with groups of people. This House also relates to one's hopes and dreams, humanitarian impulses and work for good causes.

### Twelfth House

Twelfth-house questions deal with hidden strengths and weaknesses. This House also concerns institutions, such as hospitals, jails, libraries and the armed services. It describes fears, hidden enemies, the collective unconscious, spirituality and karma.
