# Sun — The Suit of Fortune

> *Suit title page (p26).* The Sun glyph (☉︎) above the title "SUN", a horizontal rule, then the subtitle "The Suit of Fortune" in italics, with an illustration of a radiant sun-god figure driving a chariot drawn by horses; the sign of Leo (♌︎) is shown on the chariot wheel.

The twelve Sun cards represent the Sun's movement through the twelve signs of the astrological zodiac and are the most potent suit in the pack. Interpreting the combinations of planets and signs on each card allows you to assess life-spirit of the subject of a reading, revealing how his or her power to succeed in each area of life are met. The Sun cards represent identity, and masculine drive in particular. They define how we express ourselves, our need to get on in life, and how we feel at our best. Sun cards reveal the focus of our self-expression.

*Sun occupations:* Leaders, those in public office and positions of responsibility and authority; actors, performers; goldsmiths, those in the money market.

*Sun affinities:* Sunday; gold, yellow, sunflowers; lions; royalty; large mansions, palaces, rich clothing and furniture; the heart, blood circulation; gambling and sports clubs.
