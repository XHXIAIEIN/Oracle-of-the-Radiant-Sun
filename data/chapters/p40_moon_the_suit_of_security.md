# Moon — The Suit of Security

> *Suit title page (p40).* The Moon glyph (☽︎) above the title "MOON — The Suit of Security", with an illustration of a moon-goddess figure driving a chariot; the sign of Cancer (♋︎) is shown on the chariot wheel. Robed feminine figures and a hound accompany the scene over water.

The interpretation of the planet and sign combinations in this suit of cards sheds light on where and how we find emotional security and satisfaction. The Moon cards describe our emotional nature and basic needs. They also show how we experience intimacy in relationships. The Moon represents receptivity and feminine drive and helps define the way in which we assimilate experience and pass it on to others. It also reveals how we wish to be nurtured and how we nurture others, being concerned with family, traditions and links with the past. For this reason, the cards in the Moon suit offer important clues to the childhood of the subject of a reading. Depending on its sign, the card will highlight the questioner's relationship with his or her mother, the nature of the enquirer's early upbringing and how this has affected his or her life as an adult.

*Moon occupations:* sailors, salespeople, commercial travelers; nurses, carers, child-welfare workers, social workers, foster parents.

*Moon affinities:* Monday; silver, sea-green; the breasts, the stomach; opals; collections; nursery equipment; the ocean, sea journeys, boats; owls and night birds; water, milk and beer.
